About This Research

欢迎访问"人熊冲突与共存"研究网站。本站建立初衷在于希望人们注意并重视人与动物之间的生存和利益冲突， 其中人与熊之间是典型的例子。我们希望人与动物可以和谐共处，共同发展。

Welcome to the "Human-Bear Conflict and Coexistence" research website. This platform aims to raise awareness about survival and interest conflicts between humans and animals, with human-bear interactions serving as a prime example. We aspire for harmonious coexistence and mutual development between humans and wildlife.

研究说明：本站文章将翻译转述中国的人熊冲突与共存事件，目的希望全世界可以共同关注。 所有翻译转述内容均标明出处。

案例研究 2023-08-20

Bear-Human Conflicts in Yushu, Qinghai

青海玉树发生棕熊袭击事件，两头牦牛被咬死。经过4小时的"人熊博弈"，棕熊最终离开。 当地警方发布预警，提醒牧民提高安全意识。

Brown bear attacked and killed two yaks in Yushu, Qinghai. After a 4-hour standoff, the bear finally left. Local police issued warnings to alert herders.

突发事件 2023-05-17

Black Bear Attack in Jiangyou, Sichuan

四川江油发生黑熊袭击事件，造成3人死亡。搜救队伍携猎犬进山搜索，专家指出黑熊的存在反映了生态环境保护的成效， 但环保的最终目的是保护人民。

A black bear attack in Jiangyou, Sichuan resulted in 3 deaths. Search teams with hunting dogs were deployed. Experts noted that black bear presence reflects ecological protection success, but the ultimate goal is protecting people.

来源 Source: 红星新闻 (Red Star News)

政策研究 2023-08-30

How Does China Address Human-Bear Conflicts?

中国《民法典》第251条规定野生动物资源归国家所有，国家对个人损失进行补偿。青海省启动"人熊冲突预警与防范技术"科研项目。 2012-2017年间，玉树州发生20起棕熊袭击事件，造成5死6残11伤。调查显示，现有防熊方法效果不佳。

China's Civil Code Article 251 states that wildlife resources are state-owned, with government compensation for individual losses. Qinghai Province launched a "Human-Bear Conflict Early Warning and Prevention Technology" research project. From 2012-2017, Yushu Prefecture recorded 20 brown bear attacks causing 5 deaths, 6 disabilities, and 11 injuries.

安全指南 2024-05-08

Face to the Bears: Safety Guidelines

在青海、西藏、云南等地区容易发生人兽冲突，熊是典型例子。了解熊的特征至关重要：棕熊领地意识强， 嗅觉敏锐但视力差，有冬眠习性。避免冲突的关键方法：保持冷静不要逃跑（熊速度56km/h）， 不要接近熊崽，举起双手向熊表明你没有威胁。

Human-animal conflicts are common in Qinghai, Tibet, and Yunnan, with bears being a prime example. Understanding bear characteristics is crucial: strong territorial sense, keen smell but poor vision, hibernation habits. Key safety methods: stay calm and don't run (bears reach 56km/h), avoid bear cubs, raise hands to show you're not a threat.
